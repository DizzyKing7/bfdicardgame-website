Battle for Dream Island: The Card Game
1. Setup
First, when you open the box decide which side of the board you want to use (grass or space) (purely stylistic). Next, shuffle the challenge cards and place them face down next to the board. Do the same with the scene cards. They go under the marked spot on the board. Put all the stars and win tokens by the board and the Cake at Stake die goes on its marked spot. Flip the box to the side saying "Tiny Loser Chamber". Finally, give each player two scene cards, a star, and a reminder card.
1.5. Drafting Characters
For the characters, turn the character cards to the basic side, unless you want to play with the advanced side (it adds personalities to the characters!). I would recommend playing with the basic side first, then moving on to the advanced side once everyone is familiar with the game. Randomly deal 12 character cards to the table. The most recent watcher of BFDI gets to pick first, choosing one. They keep the card in front of them and put the character figure on the highest space on the START column of the board. The player to their left picks next, and so on, repeating until all characters have been chosen. Now you're ready to play (after these gameplay steps)!
2. Gameplay
Remember that there were 12 character cards? Yeah that's because it starts in the middle of the season; the merge. Every challenge follows the same 5-step format:
A. New challenge card.
Draw a challenge card. OBSERVE the icon in the top right of the challenge card. If one of your characters has a quantity of that icon, you may move them that many columns forward. (SIDE NOTE: Ranking: Rankings of characters during challenges are rightmost column to leftmost column, then topmost space in a column to bottommost space in a column.)
B. Taking turns.
The person who's character(s) are winning the challenge goes first. They may choose to either play a scene card or pass. If the play a scene card, they must follow the instructions on the card. For example: a SWAP card would allow them to swap the positions of two characters on the same column, a BOOST card would allow them to move a character forward a certain number of columns based on what they rolled on the Cake at Stake die (1-3), and a WIN TOKEN card would increase the chances of survival in Cake at Stake (more important in step D). A special boost card can only be played if the icon shown is the same as the icon on the challenge card. Special boost cards don't rely on the Cake at Stake die; it's guaranteed to move your character(s) three columns forward. Oh yeah, one more card type: DEVELOPMENT cards. These can give characters new attributes (the icons on the character cards) to help them later on. It can also strengthen existing attributes. DEVELOPMENT cards stay with the character card, and can be used in future challenges. Oh yeah, you can also pass; if at least one player plays a card, go around again until everyone has passed. Once everyone has passed, move on to step C.
C. Challenge results.
Anyone in column 3 is moved to the highest spaces in that column, then anyone in column 2 is moved to the highest spaces in level 3 still left, and finally anyone in column 1 does the same, and then the people in the start column, who'll end up in the bottom spaces of level 3.
